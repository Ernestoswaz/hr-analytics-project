# Employee Presence & Work From Home Insights

## Business Problem
The organization wants to understand employee attendance patterns and work-from-home preferences to support decisions around hybrid work policies.

## Dataset
Employee attendance data containing:
- Employee ID
- Date
- Present status
- Work From Home (WFH) status
- Sick Leave status

## KPIs Calculated
- Present %
- Work From Home (WFH) %
- Sick Leave (SL) %

## Key Insights
- Overall employee presence remained high at *91.55%* between April and June
- Work From Home (WFH) adoption averaged *10.21%*, showing a steady upward trend
- Sick Leave percentage was low at *1.08%*, indicating no negative impact from increased WFH
- In *April, presence was **94.05%* with WFH at *8.55%*
- In *May, presence dropped to **87.75%* while WFH increased to *10.80%*
- In *June, presence recovered to **90.70%* and WFH rose further to *12.88%*
- The data indicates a growing preference for a *hybrid working model*

## Approach
- Cleaned and validated attendance data
- Calculated attendance and WFH percentages using DAX
- Analyzed monthly trends to identify changes in employee work preferences
- Built an interactive Power BI dashboard to visualize attendance patterns

## Tools Used
- Power BI (DAX, Data Modeling, Visualization)
- Excel

## Business Impact
These insights help HR teams design effective hybrid work policies while maintaining high employee presence and low sick leave.
